Water Cycle

Water Cycle refers to the continuous process of movement of water within the earth and atmospher through different complex process. Sun plays the most important role in this cycle. The water in Earth evaporates into water vapour and condenses in atmosphere to form cloud and precipitate again in the form rain, snow, hailstone, sleet etc.

Different process involved in water cycle are:

1. Evaporation:

Evaporation is the process by which water changes into water vapour. This can be considered as the first step of cycle as water from different source like Rivers, ocean, lake etc. get evaporated and go to atmosphere.

water also goes to atmosphere from plants by the process of transpiration. In transpiration water molecule are removed from areal parts of plants.

2. Condensation:

In this process the evaporated water molecules again turns into liquid water. This happens when air get cooled to dew point. During this process Hailstone and snow are also formed.

3. Precipitation:

After condensation, water is now released in the form of rain, snow, hailstone, sleet. This is called precipitation.

4. Collection:

The precipitated water is collected in rivers, lakes, streams, as snow in mountains and as underground water.

5. Runoff:

Runoff is the follow of water from higher altitude to lower during rainfall or due to melting of ice when excess water cannot be absorbed by ground. Most of the runoff water get collected in river and lakes which returns to ocean while some water evaporates again.

6. Interception:

It is the retention of water by vegetation and soil which get absorbed by vegetation or get evaporated again.

7. Infiltration:

In this process water is absorbed or soaked into soil which results in underground water. If water follow through the soil and porous or fractured rocks it is called percolation

8. Discharge:

The groundwater exits into rivers, lakes, streams or directly to ocean, this process is called discharge.

Climate Change

Climate change refers to change in average weather condition of a place for long time ( several decades). Like a place getting warmer, wetter, drier for long time. The main reason of climate change is increase in greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide, nitrous oxide, CFCs, sulphur dioxide, for which we everyone are responsible. And climate change has affected almost all the natural cycles in Earth like water cycle, carbon cycle, nitrogen cycle etc.

e.g: due to increase in temperature evaporation of water increases, melting of ice-caps increases which significantly effect water cycle. Now we will study effects in water cycle in detail.

As carbon dioxide increases in atmosphere it absorb more heat energy and release less amount than it absorbs due to which temperature increases. According to National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) CO2 in our atmosphere is about 420 ppb(parts per billion) in today date, which is continuously increasing since 1955. As the observation of NOAA CO2 has increased by 50% i.e. it is 150% CO2 today than that at 1750 A.D.
